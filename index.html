Case Competitions

2015 Statistical Case Competition at Simon Fraser University

  • In September 2015, Simon Fraser University held a case competition in the Department of Statistics and Actuarial Science to commemorate the 50th anniversary of Simon Fraser University
  • We were given a dataset for a Uken mobile app game and asked to predict how much time a player spent playing the game, how much money they spent on in-app purchases and whether they would continue to play the game a month after installing it
  • In the dataset, we had demographic information about users (i.e. country they were from, gender, etc.) and also event information (i.e. did they complete level 1, did they send a gift to another user, etc.)
  • We used a random forest model and gradient boosted logistic regression model to predict the target variables and ended up getting first place out of the 7 teams involved
